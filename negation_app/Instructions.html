Instructions


Welcome to the experiment. This experiment studies decision making in pairs of individuals. It will last approximately 90 minutes. There will be {{num_playrounds}} rounds, and in each round you will be making one decision. Please read these instructions carefully. The cash payment you will receive at the end of the experiment will depend on the decisions you make. You will not learn the identity of any participants, nor will any participants learn your identity, even after the end of the experiment.

Your Role and Your Partner

There are {{num_players}} participants in today's session. Following the practice rounds, half of the participants will be randomly assigned to the role of a Sender. The other half will be randomly assigned to the role of a Receiver. Your role stays the same throughout the experiment. Each Sender will be paired with a Receiver so that {{num_groups}} pairs are formed. You remain in the same pair for the duration of the experiment.

Actions and Rewards

There are seven actions A, B, C, D, E, F, and G and seven positive rewards, 100, 90, 80, 70, 60, 50, and 40 points.

In each round, the computer randomly assigns each of the seven positive rewards to one of the seven actions. All possible assignments are equally likely. At the same time, the computer randomly determines which actions are valid. There are either two valid actions and five other actions or five valid actions and two other actions, with a {{prob_2}}% and {{prob_5}}% chance respectively. For any fixed number of valid actions, all selections of valid actions are equally likely.

Both participants in the same pair obtain the same reward. The reward is determined jointly by the action taken and the validity of that action. For your pair to receive a positive reward, the Receiver must take a valid action. The reward from any other action is 0 points.

The Sender’s Decision

If you are a Sender, you, but not the Receiver, will learn which actions are valid. The valid actions will be underlined. You will not learn the assignment of rewards to actions. After learning which actions are valid, you choose a message to send to the Receiver.

Each message is a string of up to 10 symbols. You can freely assemble your message using the symbols: *, %, #, a, b, c, d, e, f, or g by clicking the corresponding buttons. You can change your message by using the “Delete” button. Once you have finished assembling your message, click the “Send Message” button.

The first symbol in your message is costless. Each additional symbol costs both you and the Receiver {{C.COST}} points. An example of the screen a Sender will see is as follows.

The Receiver’s Decision

If you are a Receiver, you will take one of the seven actions A, B, C, D, E, F, or G. In each round, each action is randomly assigned a different positive reward from among 100, 90, 80, 70, 60, 50, and 40 points. You, but not the Sender, will see the assignment of positive rewards to actions. In addition, you see the message from the Sender, who knows which actions are valid.

If you choose a valid action, both you and the Sender will receive the positive reward assigned to that action. If you choose any other action, your reward and the Sender’s reward is 0 points. You choose an action by clicking the corresponding button. An example of the screen that a Receiver will see is as follows.

Information Feedback - for both Sender and Receiver

At the end of each round, you will be provided with a summary of what happened in that round, including which actions were valid, the Sender’s choice of message, the message cost incurred, the assignment of positive rewards to actions, the Receiver’s choice of action, and your reward for that round.

Your Cash Payment

In each round both players are endowed with {{C.ENDOWMENT}} points. The payoff in points you obtain in each round will be the [{{C.ENDOWMENT}} points endowment - the message cost + the reward for that round]. The computer will randomly select 2 rounds out of the {{num_playrounds}} rounds to calculate your cash payment. The higher payoff among the 2 rounds will be selected as your payoff of the experiment. The points you earned will be converted to US dollars at a 6:1 exchange rate, rounded up (thus, for example, 100 points correspond to $16.66). Your total cash payment will be the amount you earned in the selected round plus a $7 show-up fee.

Quiz and Practice

To ensure your comprehension of the instructions, we will give you a quiz and {{C.PRACTICE_ROUNDS}} practice rounds. We will go through the quiz after you answer it on your own. In the practice rounds, you will act as both Sender and Receiver and play against yourself. Once the practice round is over, the computer will tell you “The official rounds begin now!” and you will be randomly assigned to either the role of Sender or the role of Receiver.

Administration

Your decisions as well as your monetary payment will be kept confidential. Remember that you have to make your decisions entirely on your own. Please do not discuss your decisions with any other participants.

Upon finishing the experiment, you will receive your cash payment. You will be asked to sign your name to acknowledge your receipt of the payment. You are then free to leave.

If you have any question, please raise your hand. We will answer your question individually. If there is no question, we will proceed to the quiz now.
